== Learning Targets

Students should be able to:

* Distinguish weak AI, strong AI, human-level AI, and general AI.
* Explain major philosophical objections to AI, including informality, disability, mathematical limits, and the Chinese room.
* Explain the purpose and limitations of the Turing test.
* Discuss consciousness, qualia, and why they remain difficult questions for AI.
* Identify ethical benefits and risks of AI systems.
* Explain concerns around lethal autonomous weapons, surveillance, security, and privacy.
* Compare privacy approaches such as de-identification, k-anonymity, differential privacy, federated learning, and secure aggregation.
* Compare fairness concepts including individual fairness, group fairness, fairness through unawareness, equal outcome, equal opportunity, and equal impact.
* Explain trust, transparency, verification, validation, certification, explainable AI, and auditing.
* Analyze work automation, robot rights, unintended side effects, value alignment, specification gaming, and long-term AI safety.
