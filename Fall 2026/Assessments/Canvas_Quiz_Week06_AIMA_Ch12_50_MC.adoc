== Learning Targets

Students should be able to:

* Explain why agents need probabilistic reasoning in partially observable, nondeterministic, or complex environments.
* Distinguish logical certainty from probabilistic degrees of belief.
* Explain how probability theory addresses laziness, theoretical ignorance, and practical ignorance.
* Explain how utility theory and probability theory combine in decision theory.
* Apply the principle of maximum expected utility.
* Define sample spaces, possible worlds, events, propositions, random variables, prior probabilities, posterior probabilities, and evidence.
* Use conditional probability, the product rule, marginalization, normalization, and full joint distributions.
* Explain the probability axioms and why inconsistent degrees of belief can lead to irrational decisions.
* Explain independence, conditional independence, and why they reduce representation size.
* Apply Bayes' rule in causal and diagnostic reasoning.
* Explain Naive Bayes models and their use in text classification.
* Explain how probability improves Wumpus World decisions when logic alone leaves multiple possibilities unresolved.
